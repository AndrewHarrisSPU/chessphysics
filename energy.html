Abruptly, let's reflect on the theoretical lower bound on the amount of energy required to 'erase a bit': \(kT\ln2\), where:

  • \(k\) is the Boltzmann constant (a fascinating physical constant in its own right)
  • \(T\) is the temperature of the environment of the computation
  • \(\ln 2\) is the natural logarithm of 2

Numerically:

  • \(k = 1.38 \times 10^{âˆ’23}\) \(Joules/Kelvins\)
  • \(T = 293.15\) \(Kelvins\) (room temperature)
  • \(\ln 2 = 0.69315\)

The Landauer limit (the minimum energy to erase a bit) is, in SI units, \( 2.805 \) \( zeptoJoules \) ( zepto: \( \times 10^{-21}\)).

In theory, with some attention to binary arithmetic and optimal solutions, lower bounds can be calculated for the energy required to solve Tic-Tac-Toe, Nim, and the Towers of Hanoi.
