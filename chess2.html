With an estimated average of 157 bits per move recorded, a minumum energy of \( 157 * 2.805 \times 10^{-21}\) \(J\) is required to record a single position.

We can now calculate \(N * E_{pos}\) with:

  • \(N = 10^{120}\) positions
  • \(E_{pos} = 4.40385 \times 10^{-19}\) \(J\)

This gives \(4.40385 \times 10^{101}\) \(J\). A brute force solution for chess is computationally prohibitive, as merely recording the results of computation demands more than the mass-energy of the observable universe.
